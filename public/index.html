The Problem

Poker is one of the great strategy games.
It deserves a better currency.

Poker teaches decision-making under uncertainty — reading situations, managing risk, adapting to opponents. It builds skills that matter far beyond the table: patience, discipline, probabilistic thinking.

But for decades, poker has been inseparable from gambling. The money on the table changes the nature of the game — it stops being about strategy and starts being about stakes. We wanted to keep the brilliance of poker and remove what corrupts it.
